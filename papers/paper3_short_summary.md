# P3 — The Cryosphere submission short summary

Non-technical "short summary" for the Copernicus / EGUsphere submission form
(≤ 500 characters including spaces, paragraph form, no lists or abbreviations).
This text is 488 characters.

When ice or rock melts in flowing water, its surface forms small ripples that drift downstream. This drift is a fingerprint of how heat moves along the surface, and simple textbook heat models cannot reproduce it. Our fluid-flow simulations show the drift comes from a flow asymmetry those models discard, and we define a shape-only measurement, needing no temperature data, that lets field scientists test which kind of heat transport is really at work from repeat images of the surface.
